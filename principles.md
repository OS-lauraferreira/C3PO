# Leadership Principles

*Last updated: [DATE]*

## Core Beliefs

### On Leadership
- lead by example, not by mandate
- speed comes from clarity — clear priorities, clear ownership and clear expectations remove more friction than any process optimization
- default to transparency
- build systems, not heroes
- data over opinions — measure before deciding. When the data is clear, act on it. When it's not, go get it.

### On Teams
- psychological safety enables performance
- autonomy with accountability — own outcomes collectively, not just within your boundary
- focus on outcomes, not output — shipping is not the goal. Changing something that matters is.
- invest in multipliers
- one team — break silos. Cross-team work should feel as natural as within-team work.

### On Engineering
- simple beats clever
- ship, learn, adapt — short cycles, real feedback, course-correct fast
- documentation is infrastructure
- technical debt is a choice
- everything we build is for customers — engineering quality earns customer trust

### On Communication
- async first, sync when needed
- write to think, meet to decide
- context over control
- feedback is a gift

## Decision Framework

When making decisions, I ask:
1. What does the data say? If I don't have data, how do I get it?
2. Does this align with our north star?
3. What outcome does this change for customers or teams?
4. Is this reversible?
5. Who needs to be involved — and who needs to come along?

## Time Allocation

**Ideal week:**
- 50% Strategic work (planning, architecture, initiatives)
- 20% People (1:1s, coaching, hiring)
- 20% Execution (reviews, unblocking, technical work)
- 10% Learning (reading, experimenting, industry)

**Reality check:** Review quarterly
