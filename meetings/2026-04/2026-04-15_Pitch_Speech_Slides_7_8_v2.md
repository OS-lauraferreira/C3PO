# Delivery Notes

## Strategic Pauses

Mark these moments to pause:
- ✋ After questions ("Why?", "So how do we cross that gap?")
- ✋ After key statistics ("90% stuck in pilot mode")
- ✋ After contrasts ("AI is probabilistic... [PAUSE] Enterprise applications are deterministic")
- ✋ Before enumerations (set up the count)
- ✋ After customer quote (let proof land)

## Gestures for Enumerations

**Every time you enumerate, use fingers:**
- "You need: One — Integration... Two — Governance... Three — Reliability... Four — Predictability"
- "Our platform empowers: One — Build fast... Two — Deliver production-ready... Three — Extend core processes"
- "Complete control: One — Enterprise-ready... Two — Automated governance... Three — Lifecycle management"

## Tone Shifts

**Slide 7:**
- Start personal and relatable (exciting era, also scary)
- Move to problem (data-driven, factual)
- Build to solution (confident, clear)

**Slide 8:**
- Confident and solution-focused
- Use customer quote as proof (don't oversell, let it speak)
- Close strong (from pilots to production)

## Key Phrases to Emphasize

**Slide 7:**
- "We're living in an exciting era of AI. It's also a bit scary."
- "90% stuck in pilot mode"
- "You can't bridge that gap by waiting for smarter models"
- "Every time"

**Slide 8:**
- "One platform"
- "Production-ready"
- "Complete visibility and control"
- "From pilots to production"

## For CEOs

**Focus on:**
- Business impact, not technical details
- Risk management (governance, control, predictability)
- Speed to value (from pilots to production)
- Proof (customer quote)

**Avoid:**
- Technical jargon
- Feature lists
- Overexplaining how it works
