# SLIDE 7 — The Enterprise Gap

## Context

**Inês will ask:** "Há muito investimento em AI, mas não se vê um grande retorno."

**"There is significant investment in AI, but we are not seeing a high return."**

**And there's data behind that.**

**90% of transformative use cases are stuck in pilot mode** (McKinsey data.)

The reason is **the enterprise gap**. 

---

Many companies believe better AI will solve this. 

That assumption has created endless pilots. Disconnected experiments. No path to scale.

We're trapped on the wrong side of what we call **the enterprise gap**.

---
The core issue is:

**AI is probabilistic.** we have Statistical models. Variable outputs. 
It is Great for generating content and code.

**Enterprise applications are deterministic.** 
Predefined rules. 
Predictable results. 
Are Required for core processes and mission-critical operations.

You can't bridge that gap by waiting for smarter models.

---
## What Enterprise AI Actually Needs

is To get from experimentation to real enterprise AI, 
we need:

- **Integration** with core systems
- **Governance** and compliance
- **Reliability** in production
- **Predictability** at scale

None of those are solved by better models.

---

**The key to cross the enterprise gap is combining both approaches:**

Use the **probabilistic nature of AI** to accelerate the build phase - faster development, more experimentation.

Wrap it in a **deterministic framework** that delivers trusted AI apps. 
So you can have Apps that behave according to enterprise requirements. Every time.

# Speaking Notes

## Slide 7 — The Enterprise Gap

### Tone
- Confident, direct
- Use pauses for emphasis
- Don't rush the "Why better AI won't fix this" section—it's the turning point

### Timing
- ~2 minutes total
- Spend 30 seconds on opening (answer Inês, set the problem)
- Spend 60 seconds on why better AI won't fix it (the insight)
- Spend 30 seconds on the solution (the bridge to slide 8)

### Emphasis Points
- "90% stuck in pilot mode" → pause, let it land
- "Probabilistic vs deterministic" → slow down, this is the core concept
- "Every time" → strong close before transition

### Avoid
- Don't say "leverage" or "seamless"
- Don't oversell—stay matter-of-fact
- Don't rush—let the problem breathe before solving it
