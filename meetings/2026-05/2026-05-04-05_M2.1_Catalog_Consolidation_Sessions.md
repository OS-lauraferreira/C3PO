---

## Narrativa de referência

> The 2025 change taxonomy had 26 operation types covering 1,796 RFCs, but no formal specification governing how catalog articles should be structured or linked. Over two days, we defined that specification and ran a full consolidation analysis across all 26 types. The analysis revealed that 11 of those types were catch-alls — structurally different procedures treated as the same, with no consistent catalog article, no consistent review gate, and no consistent rollback path. The consolidation produced a redesigned taxonomy of 61 types built around specific procedures rather than broad domains, a canonical article skeleton template, and 9 governance rules — including a critical new rule: one RFC covers one operation, and follow-on operations require their own RFC. The result is a coherent, traceable change catalog ready to be built out as the operational backbone of the platform's change management process.
